Fun facts

Duck is an example of an animal name that can be used as a verb. When used to describe how humans move their head or body, it means that the way in which they do so resembles that of a duck. There is a fairly large number of other animal verbs, all of which relate to how the animal in question acts. See some examples below:

  • He got scared and rabbited away. (He got scared and ran away.)

  • I was going to go skydiving, but I chickened out. (I was going to go skydiving, but I was too frightened to do it.)

  • Be careful what you say around her; she parrots everything she hears. (Be careful what you say around her; she repeats everything she hears without thinking.)

  • I’m a model, but I love to pig out on junk food. (I’m a model, but I love to eat too much junk food.)

  • We had to snake our way through the crowd. (We had to get through the crowd moving like a snake.)

  • Stop bugging her. (Stop annoying her.)

  1. Read the sentences and do the following activities.

    She was walking the runway when her high heels broke.

    She was shopping for shoes while her husband was shopping for clothes.

    While she was pampering herself at a beauty salon, her son called.

    While James was eating a cracker, all of his friends were eating pizza and ice cream.

    a) Which action was in progress around a particular time in the past?

    1. (___) She was walking the runway [...]

    2. (___) [...] her high heels broke.

    b) Which action happened in the middle of an ongoing action in the past?

    1. (___) [...] her son called.

    2. (___) she was pampering herself [...]

    c) Which option correctly describes the verb structure used to indicate an action in progress in the past?

    1. (___) was/were + verb.

    2. (___) was/were + verb (-ing).

    d) In the examples above, when is used to indicate that actions interact with each other in the past. Which sentence correctly states this use?

    1. (___) When is used to indicate the interrupting action.

    2. (___) When may also indicate two actions in progress at the same time in the past.

    e) In relation to the use of while, it’s possible to state that

    1. (___) it indicates the interrupting action and it connects two actions in progress in the past.

    2. (___) it indicates the action that was in progress and it can also connect two actions that were in progress at the same in the past.

    Pay attention!

    • You can also use as to connect two actions in the past. Study these examples:

      – As I was walking down the street, I found this new gym.

      – I found this new gym as I was walking down the street.

    • Notice that if you start the sentence using as, you need to use a comma after the phrase. The same applies to when and while.
